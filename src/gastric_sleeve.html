Gastric sleeve

The gastric sleeve, or sleeve gastrectomy, is the most common bariatric surgery in the U.S. It's a relatively simple and safe procedure, making it suitable for most individuals with minimal risk of complications.

The gastrectomy simply removes a large portion of your stomach — about 80% — leaving behind a small, tubular portion, like a sleeve.

This naturally limits the amount of food you can eat at once, promoting a quicker feeling of fullness. Additionally, it decreases the production of hunger hormones, stabilizing metabolism, reducing appetite, and regulating blood sugar.

Advantages

01

Technically simple and shorter surgery time

02

Can be performed in certain patients with high risk medical conditions

03

Effective weight loss and improvement of obesity related conditions

Disadvantages

01

Non-reversible procedure

02

May worsen or cause new onset reflux and heart burn

03

Less impact on metabolism compared to bypass procedures
