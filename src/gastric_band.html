Gastric band

Laparoscopic surgery employs a small camera and light source to place an adjustable stomach band through minimally invasive techniques, controlling food intake by tightening or loosening as necessary.

Regular adjustments are made under X-ray guidance. This provides patients with immediate visual feedback and ensures precise filling of the band. Periodic endoscopies are required to monitor the band's position.

The Lap-BandÂ® is FDA approved for patients with a body mass index (BMI) of 30 or more and at least one obesity-related comorbidity, such as high blood pressure, heart disease, sleep apnea, Type-2 diabetes, or other conditions.

Advantages

01

Lowest rate of complications early after surgery

02

Patients can go home on the day of surgery

03

The band can be removed if needed

Disadvantages

01

The band may need several adjustments and monthly office visits during the first year

02

Slower and less weight loss than with other surgical procedures

03

Has a high rate of re-operation
